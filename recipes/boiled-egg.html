Boiled Egg

Description

A boiled egg is an egg cooked inside its shell using heat from boiling water. Depending on how long itâ€™s cooked, the texture can range from a soft, slightly runny yolk with a tender white to a fully set yolk with a firm, dense white. The shell remains intact during cooking, and when peeled away, it reveals a smooth, pale egg white encasing the rich yellow yolk. The flavor is mild and comforting, often enjoyed on its own or as part of a larger dish.

Ingredients

  • Large eggs

Steps

  1. Place eggs in a medium pot and cover with cold water by 1 inch. Bring to a rolling boil, then cover the pot and turn off the heat. Let the eggs cook, covered, for 9 to 12 minutes, depending on your desired done-ness.
  2. Transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water and chill for 14 minutes. This makes the eggs easier to peel. Peel and enjoy!
