Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering flat, wide pasta sheets with rich fillings. Traditionally, it includes a meat sauce made from ground beef or pork cooked with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs. Between the layers, you’ll often find creamy ricotta cheese or a smooth béchamel sauce, along with generous amounts of melted mozzarella and grated Parmesan.

The assembled dish is baked until the top is golden and bubbling, creating a hearty, comforting meal. There are many variations, including vegetarian options with ingredients like spinach or mushrooms, and modern takes that cater to vegan or gluten-free diets.

Ingredients

  • Ricotta cheese
  • Italian sausage
  • Mariana sauce
  • Spinach

Steps

  1. Boil pasta: In a large pot of salted water boil lasagna noodles per the recipe below.
  2. Prepare meat sauce: Cook sausage and beef with onion and garlic. Drain well, add the pasta sauce & simmer it for a few minutes to thicken.
  3. Combine cheese mixture: Stir the cheese mixture together in a bowl.
  4. Layer & bake: Layer the meat sauce and cheese mixture with lasagna noodles and bake until the top of the lasagna is golden brown.
